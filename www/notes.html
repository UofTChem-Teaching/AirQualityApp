Authors

The code for the underlying data analysis/visualization was written in R, and build into this interactive website using Shiny, by David Hall and Jessica D'eon for the CHM135H Practical Laboratory course at the University of Toronto.

Data Sources

The air quality data is taken from Environment and Climate Change Canada's (ECCC) National Air Pollution Surveillance (NAPS) Program. The displayed data is a combination of hourly O3 and NO2 measurements from 2018; it can be found freely at the NAPS Data repository here. Hourly Odd Ox measurements calculated by summing O3 and NO2 concentrations at the same hour.

Population data was assembled from the 2016 Canadian Census Profile or Wikipedia when otherwise unavailable. Classification of population size based on Statistics Canada's Population Centre and Rural Area Classification 2016. Briefly:
  • large urban population centres, consist of a population of 100,000 and over
  • medium population centres, have a population of between 30,000 and 99,999
  • small population centres, have a population of between 1,000 and 29,999
  • rural population centres, have a population below 1,000
